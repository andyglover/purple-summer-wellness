MY STORY

I received my first Reiki session in 2002, but it wasn’t until 2010 that I got my Level 1 attunement, working my way to my Level 3 attunement (master level) in 2017. Through the years, I have wanted to be a Reiki Practitioner full time, I just wasn’t sure how I was going to make that work. In 2015, I sustained an injury that left me with nerve damage and ended my 11 year career with a company I loved. Through that healing process was the first time I thought to myself "I should do reflexology" Though, too scared to make that leap, I started with another company and created a corporate career I thought I wanted. Inevitably, that was not filling my cup and the stress led me into a 6 month long migraine. I started using some reflex points I had learned casually, and it dawned on me that I had the power to change my life - I just needed the courage. So, with the support of my family and friends, I embarked on what I refer to as "My Trustfall Year". I quit my corporate job, and enrolled with Reflexology Academy Northwest .

Years ago I escaped an abusive relationship, and that experience taught me how emotional/mental trauma can have as profound an impact on the body as physical trauma can. Because of that, I am working my way through classes to become a Trauma Informed Practitioner. I cannot wait to put these skills into practice for those who need them.

I believe my experience teaching has taught me how to create a safe space for students to take chances and express themselves. And I hope that is what I can bring into my room: a safe space for you to be seen and acknowledged.

WHAT IS REFLEXOLOGY?


Reflexology is based in the idea that your whole body, every system that keeps us alive and moving, can be mapped out on the soles of your feet (as well as the palms of your hands and on your ears). By applying contact to certain areas, we can help bring the body back to homeostasis so it can perform to the best of its ability. Similar to acupuncture in the idea of helping to remove blockages, with more of a massage-type experience, Reflexology is performed with the client lying, fully clothed except socks, on a massage table or in a zero gravity chair. The pant legs may be moved up to allow for access to the calf.

Reflexology has been known to increase circulation, and increased blood flow decreases inflammation. When inflammation is decreased, we often see a reduction of pain. Other ailments that people have reported to be helped by Reflexology are: headaches/migraines, back pain, bowel movements, kidney stones, and blood pressure.

In the medical community, Reflexology is what is known as a Complementary/Alternative Modality (CAM). Other CAMs include: Acupuncture, massage, yoga, meditation, and holistic medicines - just to name a few. This also means that if you have a serious health condition, you should always seek the advice of your primary care physician or specialist.

HOW TO PREPARE FOR YOUR SESSION


PREPARE TO ARRIVE EARLY: If this is your first session, prepare to arrive 15 min early to fill out the required paperwork. If you take a lot of medications or have had a lot of ailments/diagnosis/surgeries, please be ready to list those, or bring out a printed copy I can keep for your files.

WHAT TO WEAR: No matter which service you are seeking, you will remain fully clothed*, so dress in what ever makes you comfortable. I do recommend layers as sometimes this type of work can fluctuate your body temperature. I always keep blankets on hand in case you get cold, and a fan if you get hot. My only ask is that you refrain from wearing perfume or heavily scented body products, as these can induce headaches and aggravate breathing.
*for a Reflexology session, I will remove your socks, and possibly move your pant legs up to allow access to your calf.

DURING THE SESSION: This is your time. We can talk or not. You can talk, and I can listen. I prefer if you do not engage with your cellphone, but that is ultimately up to you. I do play music, but if you prefer some sort of white/green/pink noise, that can be arranged.
Energy work as well as touch can bring to the surface many emotions we may expect, and plenty we may not! I have had people laugh, scream, and even sob uncontrollably. Some reactions may be more physical: Noises of digestion, burps, the sudden urge to use the restroom - all of it normal! Whatever course your body takes during our time together, there is no judgement. My room is a safe space for your body to express, expel, and engage what ever energy it needs in that moment.

AFTER THE SESSION: I will gently bring you back into your body and the space. Take your time sitting up. You may continue to experience fluctuation body temperature and/or emotional responses, this is completely normal. Personally, I find keeping up my water intake after a session helps me a great deal. People joke about the "emotional support water bottle" fad, but personally, I'm a fan!

FAQ


Isn’t Reflexology just a foot massage?

Whereas a Reflexology session can feel as wonderful as a foot massage, it is more than that! Relaxation is of course a major benefit that echos that of a foot massage, but when a Certified Reflexologist is working on your feet, our focus is what your feet are telling us about the rest of your body. We are feeling for differences in textures that may point to a blockage of energy somewhere in your body, and we work that point in hopes to help the stimulation the flow of blood and energy that your body is asking for. And yes, your massage therapist might have taken CE classes for reflexology, or they may also be a Certified Reflexologist on top of being an LMP. If you have a massage therapist you love, ask them about it!

Will I automatically receive Reiki during a Reflexology session?

No, you will not. One of the first things we learn in/about Reiki, is respect and consent. Practitioners are never to perform/give Reiki to a person who is unwilling or unaware. Reiki is a deeply personal experience, and should be used with the utmost respect. Just like any other body work modality - I would never just grab someone's feet and start giving them session out of the blue, so in turn, I would not focus my Reiki intention onto you without discussing it with you first.
Now, that caveat to this is: I am always running Reiki on myself, so you may feel that energy from me, but it is not focused on you. It keeps me balanced, grounded and protected.
